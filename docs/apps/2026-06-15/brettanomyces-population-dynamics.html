Culture Conditions

Starting Brettanomyces population (cells/mL). Wines are rarely sterile below 1 cell/mL.
Brett grows slowly below 12 °C; optimum ~25 °C; ethanol tolerance rises with adaptation.
Lower pH suppresses growth; most wine pH 3.2–3.8. Threshold shown on chart.
Ethanol is inhibitory above ~10%; highly ethanol-tolerant Brett strains persist in fortified wines.
Brett is a Crabtree-negative oxidative yeast; trace O₂ accelerates growth and 4-EG/4-EP production.
Molecular SO₂ is the active antimicrobial form. pH controls how much free SO₂ is molecular.
